# Why Terminal Universe Explorer?

Terminal emulators are usually treated as interchangeable tools.

They are not.

They differ in architecture, rendering models, ecosystems, and design philosophy — but that structure is rarely made visible.

---

## The problem

Most lists of terminal emulators are flat:

- alphabetical
- feature-based
- or popularity-driven

This hides the deeper relationships between them.

---

## The idea

Terminal Universe Explorer models terminal emulators as a design space.

Instead of asking:

> "Which terminal should I use?"

It asks:

> "How are terminals built, and how do they relate to each other?"

---

## The approach

Each terminal is described across multiple dimensions:

- ecosystem
- engine family
- rendering model
- surface
- status

These dimensions are not perfect, but together they reveal patterns.

---

## The goal

Not a definitive taxonomy.

Not a ranking.

But a way to:

- explore structure
- compare approaches
- understand evolution

---

## Philosophy

This is a working model.

It is intentionally:

- incomplete
- open to revision
- shaped by feedback

Clarity is more important than completeness.
